## Design Summary

Build a quiet technical editorial experience: a personal homepage that feels like a carefully maintained engineering notebook, not a marketing landing page. Borrow the reference site's strong hierarchy, compact statistics, route cards, and documentation ergonomics without copying its brand, text, or dense navigation.

The memorable motif is an indexed archive: small monospace labels, numbered sections, thin cobalt rules, and content-derived counts. The page should feel calm in both themes and remain fast on a mobile browser.

### Typography

- Body: a performance-first Chinese system sans stack. Do not download a large CJK webfont.
- Metadata and eyebrow labels: `ui-monospace`, `SFMono-Regular`, `Cascadia Code`, `Consolas`, monospace.
- Display heading: 700 weight, tight tracking, fluid size from 2.6 rem to 4.6 rem.
- Body copy: 1 rem to 1.08 rem, 1.75 line-height for Chinese reading.
- Keep prose measure near 72 characters and article content near 46 rem.

### Spacing And Layout

- Base spacing unit: 0.25 rem.
- Main landing container: 70 rem maximum.
- Article content: 46 rem maximum, with Starlight's TOC and sidebar retained.
- Section rhythm: 5 to 7 rem on desktop, 3.5 to 4.5 rem on mobile.
- Card gaps: 0.9 to 1.2 rem.
- Radius: 0.75 rem for cards, 999 px only for tags and compact actions.
- Borders: 1 px; shadows are avoided except for focus and modal elevation.

## Components

### Header

- Sticky, translucent surface with a crisp bottom border.
- Site title on the left; primary links, search, GitHub, and theme selector on the right.
- Hide text navigation below 50 rem and rely on Starlight's mobile menu.

### Hero

- Left-aligned editorial composition rather than the reference site's centered course hero.
- Small monospace status label, concise Chinese heading, one paragraph, and two actions.
- A thin vertical accent line and a compact “currently building” panel provide identity without imagery.

### Statistics

- Four content-derived cells in one shared bordered strip.
- Use tabular numerals and short labels; do not invent experience metrics.
- Collapse to a two-column grid on mobile.

### Cards

- Flat bordered surfaces with strong internal hierarchy.
- Hover uses border/accent translation of 2 px, not large shadows or scale effects.
- Project cards include state, stack, concise outcome, and links.
- Article cards emphasize date, title, description, and topic.

### Documentation

- Preserve Starlight's sidebar, page TOC, search, code blocks, and mobile navigation.
- Restyle colors and density so documentation and landing pages feel like one product.

## Page Patterns

- `/`: hero -> stats -> featured projects -> recent writing -> knowledge areas -> resume callout.
- `/projects/`: selected work first, then smaller experiments.
- `/articles/`: chronological technical writing with compact metadata.
- `/notes/`: Starlight knowledge-base index and autogenerated topic sidebar.
- `/resume/`: concise online resume with print-friendly styling.

## Content Style

- Chinese-first, direct, and specific.
- Headings are short nouns or active statements.
- Avoid inflated claims, fake metrics, and generic “passionate developer” copy.
- Unknown personal facts remain clearly marked for later replacement.
